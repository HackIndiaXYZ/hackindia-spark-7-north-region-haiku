Your Cycle Phases 🌙
4 phases. 4 versions of you. All powerful.
Menstrual Phase
Days 1–5 · The Release
Your body is shedding and renewing. This is not weakness — this is one of the most powerful biological processes in existence. Rest is productive.
🩸
🍽️ What to eat
Iron-rich foods — spinach, lentils, beetroot, jaggery to replenish iron loss
Magnesium — dark chocolate, pumpkin seeds, almonds ease cramping
Warm foods — khichdi, soups, ginger-turmeric tea — reduce inflammation
Avoid — excess salt, caffeine, cold drinks, alcohol
🧘 Movement
Child's pose, supine twist, cat-cow — relieves cramps beautifully
Gentle walking in warm clothes — improves circulation and mood
Rest is completely valid — your body is working hard internally
🌸 Self-care rituals
Hot water bottle on abdomen and lower back for 20 mins — as effective as ibuprofen
Castor oil massage on lower belly before sleep — ancient and effective
Warm Epsom salt bath with lavender oil
Journal your feelings — emotional release is healing
✓ Do this
🛌 Rest without guilt
🫖 Drink warm fluids
🤗 Ask for comfort
📔 Journal
✕ Avoid
☕ Excess caffeine
🧂 Salty food
💪 Intense workouts
🍦 Cold drinks
Follicular Phase
Days 1–13 · The Rising
Estrogen rises, energy returns, your mind gets sharper. This is your season of new beginnings — perfect for starting things, learning, creating.
🌱
🍽️ What to eat
Fermented foods — curd, idli, kimchi support estrogen metabolism
Cruciferous vegetables — broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage for hormone balance
Lighter, fresher foods — salads, sprouts, fresh fruits
Flaxseeds — sprinkle on everything, excellent phytoestrogen support
💪 Exercise
Your energy is building — try new workouts, dance, hiking
Cardio, strength training — your body responds well to intensity
Great time to set fitness goals — motivation is naturally high
✨ This phase is great for
Starting new projects — creativity and risk appetite are heightened
Social connections — you feel more open and communicative
Learning new skills — cognitive function is sharpening
Ovulatory Phase
Days 13–16 · The Peak
You are at your most magnetic. Estrogen and testosterone peak simultaneously. Confidence, charisma, and energy at their absolute height.
✨
⚡ Your superpower week
Schedule your most important meetings, presentations, interviews
Have the difficult conversations — communication skills are peak
Pain threshold is higher — ideal for intense exercise, waxing, dental work
Your glow is real — estrogen enhances skin, eyes, and voice
🍽️ What to eat
Light anti-inflammatory foods — berries, leafy greens, avocado
Zinc-rich foods — pumpkin seeds, chickpeas for healthy ovulation
Drink more water — body temperature rises slightly
Luteal Phase
Days 17–28 · The Slowdown
Progesterone rises as your body prepares. Energy drops, emotions deepen. This is an invitation to slow down — not a problem to fix.
🌕
🍽️ What to eat
Complex carbohydrates — sweet potato, oats, brown rice ease PMS cravings naturally
Magnesium — reduces anxiety, improves sleep, eases breast tenderness
B6 vitamin foods — bananas, chickpeas — natural mood support
Dark chocolate (80%+) — magnesium and it genuinely helps
💜 Emotional care
Your feelings are valid — luteal emotions are real, not "hormonal" to dismiss
Create boundaries — say no to anything that drains you
Sleep by 10 PM — melatonin works harder in this phase
Prepare your period comfort kit ahead of time
Wellness & Support 🌿
Every body is different. Every experience is valid.
🫧
PCOS
Polycystic Ovary Syndrome
▼
PCOS affects 1 in 10 women. Your ovaries produce excess androgens causing irregular periods, acne, weight changes. It is manageable — millions live full, vibrant lives with it.
🥗
Low-GI diet reduces insulin resistance which drives most PCOS symptoms
🏃
30 mins moderate daily exercise significantly reduces testosterone levels
💊
Myo-inositol supplement has strong research backing for PCOS
😴
Consistent sleep schedule — disrupted sleep directly worsens hormones
🔥
Endometriosis
You are not dramatic. The pain is real.
▼
Endo tissue grows outside the uterus causing severe pain. It takes 7–10 years on average to diagnose. Your pain was never "just cramps."
🔥
Heat therapy is one of the most effective immediate relief tools
🥦
Anti-inflammatory diet — reduce red meat, dairy, refined sugar
📊
Track pain with Luna — detailed logs help doctors diagnose faster
🛌
Rest without guilt during flares — this is a chronic illness
🌧️
PMDD
Severe premenstrual dysphoric disorder
▼
PMDD is severe PMS that significantly disrupts daily life — intense mood swings, depression, rage, anxiety in the 1–2 weeks before your period. It is a recognised medical condition.
📅
Track symptoms daily — show your doctor a clear pattern across 2–3 cycles
🏃
Daily cardio is clinically proven to reduce PMDD severity
💊
SSRIs taken only in the luteal phase — discuss with your doctor
🤝
Tell the people who love you — support makes a measurable difference
Immediate cramp relief 🌿
🔥
Heat therapy
Hot water bottle on lower abdomen for 15–20 min. Relaxes uterine muscles. As effective as ibuprofen for many.
🫁
4-7-8 Breathing
Inhale 4 counts, hold 7, exhale 8. Activates parasympathetic nervous system and reduces pain perception in minutes.
🌿
Ginger tea with jaggery
Fresh ginger has prostaglandin-inhibiting properties — same mechanism as NSAIDs, but natural.
🧘
Child's Pose (Balasana)
Hold 3–5 min. Gently compresses abdomen relieving uterine cramping. Add a rolled blanket under belly for extra relief.
When to see a doctor ⚠️
⚠️
Pain that stops your day
Period pain should not regularly force you to miss work or school. If it does — this is a medical issue worth investigating.
⚠️
Soaking more than 1 pad per hour
Menorrhagia can cause anaemia and has treatable causes. You don't have to live with it.

💬
Tell your people
Sharing your cycle phases with a partner transforms understanding. "I'm in luteal" communicates more than any argument can.
📝
Cycle journaling
Write in each phase. Menstrual writing is often your most honest and insightful.
🌿
Adaptogens
Ashwagandha, shatavari, tulsi — centuries of Ayurvedic evidence for hormonal balance. Consult before starting.

First Period
Everything you weren't taught in school
▼
Getting your first period usually happens between ages 9–16. First cycles are often irregular for 1–2 years. Everything you're feeling is normal.
📅
Track from Day 1 — count from the first day of bleeding to the next
💊
For cramps: ibuprofen or mefenamic acid. Take with food, not empty stomach.
🛍️
Try different products — pads, tampons, period underwear, cups — find yours
🌕
Perimenopause & Menopause
The next chapter, not the end
▼
Typically begins in 40s, lasts 4–10 years. Hot flashes, irregular periods, sleep changes, mood shifts — all real, all manageable.
🥗
Phytoestrogens in soy, flaxseed, and chickpeas naturally ease symptoms
🏋️
Strength training protects bone density which declines with estrogen
🩺
HRT is safe for most women and dramatically improves quality of life
👶
Postpartum
Your body just did something extraordinary
▼
Periods return 6–12 weeks postpartum if not breastfeeding, up to 18 months while breastfeeding. Your cycle may be different than before.
🍽️
Continue prenatal vitamins — recovery and breastfeeding demand high nutrition
💜
Postpartum depression affects 1 in 7 women. It is a medical condition. Please reach out.
📅
Don't rely on breastfeeding as contraception — ovulation can happen before your first period back

The Luna Privacy Manifesto
Our promise to you, in plain language.
📱 Your data lives on your phone first
Everything you log in Luna — your cycle dates, your mood, your symptoms, your journal, your check-in answers — is stored on your device first, in your browser's local storage. Not on a server. Not in a database that someone else controls. On your phone. Luna does not need a server to function for you personally.

The only data that ever leaves your device is the small slice you choose to share with your connected person through the Room Code system.
🔑 The Room Code — How the Connection Works
When you connect to Aura, you share a 6-character Room Code. Think of it as a private key to your door. Only the person who has this exact code can see your shared data — not us, not anyone else, not even by accident.

The Room Code is generated randomly on your device. It is not stored on our servers linked to your identity. We do not know what your code is. We cannot look it up. The connection exists only between your two devices, passing through Firebase — Google's real-time database — in transit. It does not sit in a readable database anywhere.
👁 You control every single thing they see
Every data point that reaches Aura goes through your Privacy Controls. You can share your phase without your mood. You can share your symptoms without your flow intensity. Each toggle is a real filter — not just a display setting, but a data-level switch that stops that field from being written to the shared room entirely.

When you pause sharing with the Ghost Switch, zero data is written to the shared room. Your person's app shows nothing. They are not notified. The silence is invisible.
🚫 What we do not do
We do not sell your data. Not to advertisers, not to pharmaceutical companies, not to insurance providers, not to anyone.

We do not show you advertisements. Luna has no ad system, no tracking pixels, and no third-party analytics embedded in the app.

We do not profile you for any purpose other than personalising your own experience. The data you log stays inside your experience of Luna — it does not leave to build profiles, train public models, or be traded in any form.

We do not retain your data if you reset Luna. When you reset, everything is wiped from your device and the shared room is deleted from Firebase simultaneously.
🤝 The Relationship Promise
Luna was built on a single conviction: the most intimate data a woman has is her body data. It deserves more protection, more respect, and more control than any other type of personal information.

You connected your person because you chose to. Every piece of what they see, you authorised. Every silence, you created. The system does not have opinions about what you share or don't — it only does exactly what you tell it to do.

Your body. Your data. Your rules. Always.
